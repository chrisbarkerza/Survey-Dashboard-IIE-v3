Survey Overview

Key Finding: Significant self-selection bias with respondents showing exceptionally high digital literacy (64% 'High' or 'Advanced' IT proficiency) and GenAI adoption (77% for work).

Critical Note: The remaining ~88% of staff may hold different perspectives and require foundational support.

Critical Crisis

Academic Integrity Crisis: Over 90% of academic staff are concerned about student misuse, with widespread suspected abuse.

Response Gap: Despite this awareness, only 36% have adapted their assessment designs.

Key Strategic Insights

The Efficiency Imperative

80% agreement on efficiency benefits drives adoption across all staff groups. Staff are eager to reduce administrative burdens and streamline workflows.

Academic Integrity Crisis

Most urgent threat: 90%+ staff concerns about student misuse with suspected widespread abuse, yet only 36% have adapted assessments.

The 'Freemium' Risk

78.8% use free tools, exposing the institution to security vulnerabilities, data privacy risks, and limiting access to advanced features.

High Adoption Among Digitally Literate Staff

The survey reveals exceptionally high GenAI adoption rates, with 77% using it for work and 86.7% for personal use. This reflects significant self-selection bias toward early adopters with high digital literacy.

Important: These results likely represent ~12% of staff (early adopters). The remaining ~88% may require different approaches and foundational support.

Critical Implementation Gap

While academic staff show high support for GenAI in teaching principles, there's a significant gap between supported principles and actual implementation, particularly in high-stakes activities.

Largest Gap: Student Assessment & Feedback (36.8% gap) due to concerns over accuracy, ethics of AI-assisted grading, and reliability.

Strong Administrative Support

Non-academic staff strongly support GenAI integration into administrative functions, with 81.9% supporting Operations & Support applications.

Key Opportunity: Institution has yet to leverage GenAI for deep process automation or advanced data analysis. Significant untapped potential exists.

What Staff Want From The IIE

Survey respondents identified the most important types of assistance The IIE could provide to support their GenAI usage. Training emerges as the top priority, followed by financial and infrastructure support.

Key Insight: The top three requests (training, licenses, approved apps) all exceed 75% support, indicating strong consensus on institutional needs.

Strategic Recommendations Framework

The IIE must move swiftly from ungoverned, ad-hoc adoption to a structured, strategic deployment. We recommend a strategy built on three pillars:

Pillar 1: Governance & Academic Integrity (URGENT PRIORITY)

Assessment Modernization Initiative

Timeline: 3-12 Months (Phased)

Launch cross-divisional task force to urgently redesign assessments, prioritizing authentic assessment over formats vulnerable to GenAI.

Ethical Use & Disclosure Policy

Timeline: 1-3 Months

Develop comprehensive policies for acceptable use, data privacy, and mandatory disclosure of GenAI assistance.

AI Detection & Response Framework

Timeline: 3 Months

Deploy advanced AI-detection tools with clear protocols for investigation and response, emphasizing education over punishment.

Call to Action

This framework requires institutional investment in infrastructure, policy development, and organizational change management. The IIE must act decisively to transform from crisis to competitive advantage.

Next Steps: We recommend immediate engagement with specialized consultancy expertise to ensure successful implementation of this comprehensive GenAI strategy.
